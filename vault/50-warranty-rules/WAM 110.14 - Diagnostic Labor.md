# WAM 110.14 - Diagnostic Labor

Diagnostic labor must be supported in the Diagnostics field with the diagnostic methods, tools, and procedures performed.

## Claimable examples

- Service Advisor use to identify the failed component.
- Multimeter or electrical testing.
- Special tests required to eliminate possibilities.
- Complex electrical testing for shorts, opens, intermittent issues, and harness faults.
- Recalibration when it corrects a warrantable condition and is not normal scheduled maintenance.
- Reasonable time to verify and validate the failure.

## Not claimable as diagnostic labor

- DTAC case entry or using a DTAC case number as the only justification.
- Building parts lists or estimates.
- Searching DTAC solutions.
- Visual inspections for obvious external leaks.
- Visual oil inspection or taking oil samples.
- Obvious failures where repair is readily apparent.
